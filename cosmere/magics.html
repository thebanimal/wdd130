Investiture

“Investiture is the guiding principle behind all of the magic systems within the Cosmere,”5 and as such, can be found on every world of the Cosmere (so far). “When a person is Invested, they’re actively tapping into their planet or realm’s specific form of magic and channeling it.”5

Allomancy – Mistborn

Allomancy is the first of 3 known magic systems on Scadrial, the world of the Mistborn Series. This power requires a person to ingest “different metals and metabolizing (‘burning’) them to achieve various effects.”5 There are two main types of Allomancers, Mistings and Mistborn. “Mistings are those who can only metabolize one metal and therefore access a single power of Allomancy, whereas a Mistborn is one who can metabolize all sixteen metals and their alloys to gain access to the full range of allomantic abilities.”5

The different types of metals used and their effects are:

  • Iron – “Allows the Allomancer to pull on nearby sources of metal.”5
  • Steel – “Allows the Allomancer to push on nearby sources of metal.”5
  • Tin – “Increases the sensitivity and capacity of the Allomancer’s five senses.”5
  • Pewter – “Increases the Allomancer’s physical strength, speed, durability, and enhances the body’s ability to heal.”5
  • Zinc – “Allows the Allomancer to riot (enflame) the emotions of those nearby them.”5
  • Brass – “Allows the Allomancer to soothe (dampen) the emotions of those nearby them.”5
  • Copper – “Creates an invisible cloud around the Allomancer which hides all within it from those who are burning Bronze.”5
  • Bronze – “Allows an Allomancer to ‘hear’ pulses given off by other Allomancers who are burning metals.”5
  • Chromium – Allows an Allomancer to wipe the reserves of another Allomancer by touching them.
  • Nicrosil – Allows an Allomancer to wipe the reserves of another Allomancer and giving “a huge (perhaps unexpected) burst of power”5 by touching them.
  • Aluminum – Wipes the reserves of the Allomancer.
  • Duralumin – Wipes the reserves of the Allomancer “releasing an enormous burst of power related to those metals.”5
  • Cadmium – Creates “a bubble around the Allomancer,”5 in which time passes more slowly.
  • Cerrobend – Creates “a bubble around the Allomancer,”5 in which time passes more quickly.
  • Gold – “Allows an Allomancer to see a vision of possible other lives they could have led, and different paths they could have taken.”5
  • Electrum – “Allows an Allomancer to see a vision of possible paths they could take in the future. Usually limited to a few seconds.”5

These last two metals are known as the God metals. They are extremely rare and don’t count toward the 16 Allomantic metals.

  • Atium – “Grants the Allomancer an expansive vision of the future and enhances their mind to accept, process, and hold more information.”5
  • Larasium – “Transforms a person into an Allomancer or drastically improves their Allomantic powers.”5

Feruchemy – Mistborn

Feruchemy is the second of the 3 known magic systems in the Mistborn Series. “A feruchemist can actually store different aspects of themselves to tap into at a later time”5 in bits of metal called metalminds. To do this they reduce that aspect for a time to boost that aspect later.

The different types of metalminds and what they can store are:

  • Iron – “Stores physical weight.”8
  • Steel – “Stores physical speed.”8
  • Tin – “Stores senses.”8
  • Pewter – “Stores physical strength.”8
  • Zinc – “Stores mental speed.”8
  • Brass – “Stores warmth.”8
  • Copper – “Stores memories.”8
  • Bronze – “Stores wakefulness.”8
  • Chromium – “Stores fortune.”8
  • Nicrosil – “Stores investiture.”8
  • Aluminum – “Stores identity.”8
  • Duralumin – “Stores connection.”8
  • Cadmium – “Stores breath.”8
  • Cerrobend – “Stores energy.”8
  • Gold – “Stores health.”8
  • Electrum – “Stores determination.”8

These last metal is known as one of the God metals. It is extremely rare and doesn't count toward the 16 Feruchemical metals.

  • Atium – Stores age.

Hemalurgy – Mistborn

Hemalurgy is the third of the 3 known magic systems in the Mistborn Series. “A hemalurgist, using special metal spikes, can pierce a person with allomantic or feruchemical abilities and—depending on where they pin the spike—can steal the allomantic or feruchemical abilities of that person for themself.”5 Hemalurgy can also steal “innate human strength”9 and “almost any type of Investiture.”9 It is “usable by anyone with the right knowledge,”4 regardless of world.

The types of spikes and what they can steal are:

  • Iron – “Steals strength.”9
  • Steel – “Steals physical allomantic powers.”9
  • Tin – “Steals senses.”9
  • Pewter – “Steals physical feruchemical powers.”9
  • Zinc – “Steals emotional fortitude.”9
  • Brass – “Steals cognitive feruchemical powers.”9
  • Copper – “Steals mental fortitude, memory, and intelligence.”9
  • Bronze – “Steals mental allomantic powers.”9
  • Chromium – “Might steal destiny.”9
  • Nicrosil – “Steals investiture.”9
  • Aluminum – “Removes all powers.”9
  • Duralumin – “Steals connection/identity.”9
  • Cadmium – “Steals temporal allomantic powers.”9
  • Cerrobend – “Steals spiritual feruchemical powers.”9
  • Gold – “Steals hybrid feruchemical powers.”9
  • Electrum – “Steals enhancement allomantic powers.”9

These last two metals are known as the God metals. They are extremely rare and don’t count toward the 16 Hemalurgic metals.

  • Atium – “Steals any power.”9
  • Larasium – “Steals all abilities.”9

Breath – Warbreaker

Breath, also called BioChroma, is the primary magic system on Nalthis, the world on which Warbreaker takes place. “Breath is the power of life, essentially, and the more Breaths you have, the more power over that life you have. A person is born with a single Breath, but through many means, that person can add Breaths to their being. The more Breaths you have, the more abilities you gain from them.”5 The abilities manifest in ten stages called heightenings. “Those who have Invested themselves with Breaths can actually re-invest those Breaths into inanimate objects, then set them tasks to perform.”5

The different heightenings are:

  1. 50 breaths needed – “Aura recognition.”12
  2. 200 breaths needed – “Perfect Pitch.”12
  3. 600 breaths needed – “Perfect color recognition.”12
  4. 1,000 breaths needed – “Perfect life sense.”12
  5. 2,000 breaths needed – “Agelessness.”12
  6. 3,500 breaths needed – “Instinctive awakening.”12
  7. 5,000 breaths needed – “Invested breath recognition.”12
  8. 10,000 breaths needed – “Command breaking.”12
  9. 20,000 breaths needed – “Greater awakening, audible command.”12
  10. 50,000 breaths needed – “Color distortion, perfect invocation, mental commands, possibly others.”12

“The Heightenings, while commonly thought of as discrete levels, are in fact merely markers of a continuous progression. Each Breath gathered increases an Awakener's power.”12

Aon Dor – Elantris

Aon Dor, also called just the Dor, is one of the main magic systems used on Sel, the world on which Elantris takes place. “The Dor is a massive realm of power hidden from the world which can only be accessed through various linguistic devices and forms, and/or specific movement or shapes.”5 Those “Invested with a connection to the Dor”5 are able to draw Aons, symbols which the people of Sel use as their written language, in the air, creating a variety of effects based on what they write and how they write it. Others “practice specific martial arts, whose forms satisfy something in the Dor.”5 There is even “a group of monks in the mountains [that] actually grow their bones into specific shapes, tapping into the Dor through the twisted symbols within their own bodies.”5

Forgery – The Emperor’s Soul

Forgery, which also takes place on Sel, is the main form of magic in The Emperor’s Soul. “A different means to access the Dor, Forgery is all about rewriting the history of an object, and then using linguistic shapes in the form of Soulstamps to activate those rewrites.”5 Special Soulstamps, called Essence Marks, “allow the forger to change their own history, rewriting it to give themselves specific abilities, skills, information, and so on.”5

Surgebinding – The Stormlight Archive

Surgbinding is one of the “many, many different magic systems operating”5 on Roshar, the world on which The Stormlight Archive takes place. “On Roshar… there are ten fundamental forces of the universe, and these are known as surges. A Surgebinder can access two of these surges each through the bond they develop with a spren, a sentient force of life or emotion. The spren helps them tap into the Investiture of the planet, a substance known as Stormlight.”5 To use these surges, “the surgebinder inhales and holds onto the Stormlight, and uses it as fuel to for their abilities.”5

The ten types of surgebinders and the surges they use are:

  • Windrunners – Adhesion and Gravitation
  • Skybreakers – Gravitation and Division
  • Dustbringers – Division and Abrasion
  • Edgedancers – Abrasion and Progression
  • Truthwatchers – Progression and Illumination
  • Lightweavers – Illumination and Transformation
  • Elsecallers – Transformation and Transportation
  • Willshapers – Transportation and Cohesion
  • Stonewards – Cohesion and Tension
  • Bondsmiths – Tension and Adhesion

Sand Mastery – White Sand

Sand mastery is the main magic system on Taldain, the world on which White Sand takes place. “Sand mastery makes use of the white sand found on Taldain's Dayside.”13 “It involves manipulation of white sand at the cost of the user's internal water supply. In addition, white sand that has been manipulated will turn black, becoming unresponsive,”13 signaling that it has been drained of its Investiture. “Black sand turns white again after four hours of sun exposure.”13

Aviar – Sixth of the Dusk

Aviar are special birds on First of the Sun, the planet on which Sixth of the Dusk takes place. “Most Aviar are relatively large birds, approximately three fists tall.”6 “Aviar are more intelligent than ordinary animals. Wild Aviar exhibit behaviors similar to other birds.”6 “Juvenile birds can gain magical powers, or "talents", by consuming special [Invested] worms.”6 Not many of these talents are known, but “the power obtained by Aviar is different for each species of bird.”6 “Aviar are able to share their powers with humans through a Spiritual Connection.”6 Two of the known abilities are the “ability to conceal their mental activity and those of others around them”6 and the ability “to glimpse into the future… [seeing] potential deaths of a person, and [manifesting] these potential deaths as visions of corpses wherever they could be killed.”6

Shades - Shadows for Silence in the Forests of Hell

Shades are “Spirits of dead Threnodites,”7 residents of the planet Threnody, the planet on which Shadows for Silence in the Forests of Hell takes place. “The shades haunt the Forests preying on travelers and can only be defeated with silver. The shades are dangerous and feared by all inhabitants of the Forests, as their touch can cripple and disfigure and may even kill a person and turn them into a shade.”7 The Threnodites made “the Simple Rules” to avoid attracting shades. The Simple Rules forbid the lighting any kind of fire,”7 the drawing of blood, and “moving quickly at night.”7 “Violation of the Simple Rules is the only known way to cause a Shade to enrage.”7 “Shades are almost entirely incorporeal, able to pass through solid substances like walls. The most dangerous of a shade's ability is its touch. A touch from a shade withers and blackens flesh, causing it to pull in towards the bone.”7 “The withering touch of a shade can only be reversed in a few specific ways. Application of silver powder may reverse a person's withering, either by application to the affected area or swallowing the powder itself.”7 “Shades can also be wounded with weapons made silver”7
